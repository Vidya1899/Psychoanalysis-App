Automated Graphology

Graphology or Handwriting Analysis as is often called, is a modern form of psychology that identifies personality traits and human behaviour through handwriting. Just like an individualâ€™s bio-metric, facial features and voice tone, handwriting too is individualistic and unique! We posit that since handwriting styles are statistically unique, a computer could possibly reveal the author's behavioural traits analysing the handwriting, with high accuracy. The text region, lines, words and characters are extracted, studied and analysed to detect any common patterns.

INTRODUCTION

End to end procedure

The handwriting sample is camera scanned and run through a series of computer vision processing followed by predefined rule based classification to determine traits of the individual. Various traits like - Level of socially outgoing, Degree of tolerance, Practicality, Optimism and Enthusiasm can be determined in this level of analysis.

Mahatma Gandhi's Writing

The way the 't' patterns are formed in his writing goes on to reveal the tolerant nature of Mr.Gandhi. He is well known for his non-violence movement in the freedom struggle of India against the British. The inert strength and high degree of tolerance is the major highlight of his writing.

Sir Thomas Alva Edison's Writing

The 't' bar and its repeated extensive length and piercing pattern into the next words tells a great deal about Sir Thomas Alva Edison. It indicates high degree of enthusiasm towards life. Even at the face of failure, the drive to keep going is rare and is seen explicitly in Edison's writing.
